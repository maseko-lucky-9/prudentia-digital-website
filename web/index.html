Under the hood. React 19 plus Next.js, PostgreSQL, Drizzle or Prisma, Cloudflare or Vercel at the edge, Playwright, OpenTelemetry, Sentry for error tracking.

How We Ship

Production web is a different discipline.

Perf budget in CI — measured, not assumed

Every pull request is checked against an LCP and INP budget. A regression over 10% fails the build before review. Field data from CrUX is reviewed monthly, not at launch only.

If LCP regresses, the build fails.

Accessibility AA from the first commit

WCAG 2.1 AA is wired into CI with axe-core. Keyboard navigation, contrast, focus order and form labelling are gates, not afterthoughts. Manual screen-reader checks ship at every milestone.

Built for everyone, gated by tests.

POPIA-native data flows

Every personal-information field has a documented purpose, a retention class, and a deletion endpoint. Audit logs are tamper-evident. The Information Regulator's R5 million DBE fine is the floor we engineer above.

No collection without a documented purpose.

Feature flags and progressive delivery

Every change ships behind a flag. Dark-launch to 1%, scale on health signals, kill-switch in milliseconds. LaunchDarkly customers report 76% lead-time reduction and 82% fewer rollback incidents — we use the same pattern.

Ship Friday, sleep Saturday.

OpenTelemetry from day one

Traces, structured logs, metrics. P50, P95 and P99 dashboards. We see how the app behaves in production, not how it behaved in a demo. Latency, error rate and saturation are first-class signals.

You see it. You own it.

Common Questions

Answers before you book

How long does a web engagement take?

A Discovery Sprint is one to two weeks. A Product Build is typically six to twelve weeks for an MVP-to-production scope. Engineering Retainers are monthly with no fixed end date. Indicative ranges are shared in the discovery session.

Do you sign NDAs?

Yes. We send a counter-signed mutual NDA within 24 hours of a discovery session, before any of your confidential data is shared. If you have your own NDA template, we will review and counter-sign that instead.

How do you handle our customers' data under POPIA?

Every form has a documented purpose and lawful basis. Default retention is configured per data class. Deletion is a tested endpoint, not a wishlist item. We document operator agreements with any third-party processor. POPIA enforcement is real — the Information Regulator issued an R5 million administrative fine against the Department of Basic Education in November 2024 — and our defaults engineer above that floor.

What does pricing look like?

Three engagement shapes — Discovery Sprint, Product Build, and Engineering Retainer. Sprints and Builds are fixed-fee. Retainers are monthly. We price on scope and outcome, not hourly, because hourly pricing rewards slow delivery. Indicative ranges are shared after the discovery session.

Where will our application be hosted?

We default to Cloudflare or Vercel at the edge with a PostgreSQL database in a region that fits your data-residency posture (typically af-south-1 for SA workloads). If you require on-premise or in-VPC hosting, we deploy there instead. For SA-regulated financial services we document the hosting region and any material cloud agreements so you can notify SARB under PA Directive D3/2018 if your environment requires it.

Why React/Next.js and Postgres — and not the framework I read about last week?

Because we ship the boring stack on purpose. React is used by 41.6% of professional developers and PostgreSQL by 49% (Stack Overflow 2024 Developer Survey). Depth of talent and tooling is decisive when you are operating a system for years. We pick boring fundamentals so the budget lands on the parts that move your business outcome, not on framework rewrites.
