What we build. The topology — active-passive or active-active. The data-replication strategy. Quarterly failover drills with documented RPO and RTO. The runbook your CIO will be asked to produce by their regulator.

Example use case. An SA financial-services payments processor moves to active-passive across Cape Town and Johannesburg. Documented RPO under 5 minutes, RTO under 30 minutes. Joint-Standard-aligned and audit-ready.

How We Ship

Reliability is engineered, not assumed.

SLO-first, alert-second

We define service-level objectives before we wire alerts. Pages fire on burn rate of the error budget, not on raw symptoms. False-positive rate stays low. MTTR drops. On-call sleeps.

Page on what matters.

Infrastructure-as-Code is the API

Terraform modules are versioned, reviewed, and applied via pipeline. No console clicks in production. State is auditable. Rollback is a git revert. Every change is traceable to the engineer who approved it.

No clicks in prod.

Limit blast radius by default

Multi-AZ for state. Multi-region for critical paths. IAM segregation. Feature flags. Canary or blue-green deploys. The worst day is bounded. Gartner pegs average downtime at $5 600 per minute — financial services much higher.

The worst day is bounded.

Observability wired before launch

OpenTelemetry spans, structured logs, RED and USE metrics, distributed traces, synthetic probes from outside the network. You see how the system behaves in production, not how it behaved in a demo.

You see it. You own it.

Secrets live in a vault

External Secrets Operator with HashiCorp Vault, AWS Secrets Manager, or Azure Key Vault. Rotation automated and audited. mTLS between internal services. No secrets in env files, ever.

Secrets in a vault, not in a repo.

Runbooks live in the repo, on-call is humane

Every pageable alert links to a markdown runbook in the same repo as the code. Rotation respects the Google SRE benchmark of two to three actionable incidents per shift. On-call is a craft, not a punishment.

Direct line, no relay.

Common Questions

Answers before you book

How long is a typical cloud engagement?

An Architecture Review runs two to four weeks. A Migration or Greenfield Platform Build runs eight to sixteen weeks. Platform Retainers are monthly with a 90-day minimum.

Where does our data live, and how do you handle POPIA?

South African data stays in the AWS Cape Town or Azure South Africa North region by default. Cross-border replication requires a documented POPIA Section 72 basis — substantially-similar protection, consent, contract necessity, or a binding agreement — and a signed cloud-services agreement. Personal information is only transferred under one of these grounds, never on default infrastructure behaviour.

Do you provide on-call coverage and SLAs?

Yes, on the Platform Retainer tier. We share a rotation with your engineers, define SLIs and SLOs against the services we operate, and respond on a target MTTR. SLAs are written and signed, with credits for missed targets. Rotation density is held to the Google SRE benchmark of two to three actionable incidents per shift.

Can you build active-active across multiple regions?

Yes. We design the topology — active-passive or active-active — handle the data-replication strategy and consistency trade-offs, and run quarterly failover drills with documented RPO and RTO. For SA financial services this maps directly to Joint Standard 2 of 2024 quarterly resilience-testing obligations.

What is the pricing model — fixed-fee or time-and-materials?

Architecture Review is fixed-fee. Migration or Greenfield Build is fixed-fee per phase with named deliverables. Platform Retainer is monthly with a documented capacity envelope. We price on outcome and scope, not on hours, because hourly billing rewards slow delivery and punishes the parts we have automated.

How do we exit if we want to take this in-house?

Everything lives in your repos, your cloud account, your secrets vault, your monitoring stack. We document the handover, run two weeks of shadow on-call with your team, then exit. No proprietary lock-in. You keep the runbooks, the Terraform modules, and the dashboards. Vendor portability is a design constraint, not an afterthought.
