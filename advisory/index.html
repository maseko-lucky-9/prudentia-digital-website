What We Do

Capability 01

Transformation Consult

The problem. You are about to commit R20m to a transformation programme. Every vendor in the room is enthusiastic. No one in the room is neutral. The board wants a second opinion before it signs.

What we deliver. One 90-minute structured session with the founder. Inputs: current architecture diagram, vendor shortlist, board mandate, budget envelope. Outputs: a one-page memo with the three questions the board has not yet asked, two named risks, and a recommended decision sequence.

Example. A SA-Mid retailer's executive committee paused a R28m ERP signing after a 90-minute consult surfaced an unresolved data-residency conflict with SARB Directive D3/2018. The pause cost a week. The fix cost the project months of post-signing renegotiation.

How we work. Compression Planning (six-step Fortune 500 framework), DACI for decision rights, a written one-pager. No slides.

Capability 02

Architecture Review with written ADRs

The problem. Your architecture has no architect. Decisions have been made but never written down. New engineers re-litigate them every quarter. The next CTO candidate calls the system a black box.

What we deliver. A four-to-six-week engagement. We document the current system in TOGAF-aligned views and surface every undocumented decision. Each material call lands as a written ADR — alternatives considered, constraint, decision, reversal cost.

Example. A SA-FS lender's microservices estate had 47 services and zero ADRs. After the review, 31 ADRs were committed to a private repo and the next architect onboarded in two weeks instead of two months.

How we work. TOGAF 10, the joelparkerhenderson ADR template, AWS Prescriptive Guidance ADR process. Written deliverables only.

Capability 03

The problem. You are choosing between AWS, Azure and GCP. Every conversation routes you toward the vendor running the meeting. Gartner data is blunt: 67% of vendor-led-advisory clients adopt the vendor's own platform, against 23% for independent advisors.

What we deliver. A vendor-neutral workload evaluation that ignores feature parity and benchmark theatre. We score on control, cost behaviour, operations, team skillset and organisational reality. Output: an ADR, a TCO model, and a SARB Directive D3/2018-ready board memo.

Example. A SA insurer cut projected three-year cloud spend by 34% by avoiding an all-platform commit and choosing a hybrid AWS-primary, on-prem-resident pattern for core policy data. The board approved on first read.

How we work. AWS Well-Architected, Azure Cloud Adoption Framework, a written TCO model, ADRs, SARB-aligned governance memo.

What we deliver. A six-week diagnostic. We document every system, map every integration, quantify technical debt and TCO, and produce a phased roadmap. What to replace first, in what order, at what cost, with exit criteria, reversal points, and success metrics named at each stage.

Example. A SA-Mid manufacturer's stalled SAP ECC to S/4HANA programme restarted on a 12-month phased plan. 18-month residual cost dropped 38%, with a single Driver named per workstream under DACI.

How we work. TOGAF, DACI, a written roadmap with named reversal points, an ADR per material decision.

Capability 05

Steering Retainer

The problem. Your programme is moving. The steering committee needs a senior, neutral voice once a month — to push back and ask the unpopular question. To write the ADR no one else wants to write.

What we deliver. A monthly fixed-fee retainer. The founder attends one steering meeting per month, reviews two artefacts (proposal, vendor response, design doc), and writes one ADR per quarter on a strategic decision. Exit criteria documented at signing.

How We Ship

Six written commitments, not slogans.

Discovery first

No recommendation without a current-state document we co-signed. We do not recommend what we have not seen, and we do not start an engagement on someone else's slide deck.

See it. Document it. Then advise.

Vendor neutrality

No platform reseller agreements, no implementation fees from named vendors, no kickback structures. If the right answer is do not buy, we write that. Gartner: 67% vendor-led adoption versus 23% for independents.

No partnerships. No platform routing.

Written ADRs, always

Every material decision becomes an Architecture Decision Record in a repository you own. Alternatives, constraint, decision, reversal cost. One page each. Still searchable in three years.

A repo of decisions, not a folder of slides.

Measurable success criteria

Every engagement names its success metric at scoping, not at delivery. If the metric is "the board can approve the cloud RFP this quarter", we write that down before the engagement begins.

Defined at the start. Measured at the end.

Exit criteria, documented

We do not leave when the consult ends — we leave when the named exit criteria are met. If neither party can write the exit criteria at signing, we do not start the engagement.

Defined exit. No indefinite retainer.

Common Questions

Answers before you book

How long is a typical engagement?

Three options, each scoped at signing. A Transformation Consult is one to three days of senior time; output is a written one-page memo. A Roadmap and ADRs engagement runs four to six weeks. A Steering Retainer is monthly, with documented exit criteria.

Do you implement, or just advise?

We advise and we write. We do not implement, and we do not earn fees from the vendors you choose. If you need an implementation partner, the ADR we produce names two or three candidates by capability fit, and your team selects.

How is vendor neutrality different from what Big 4 firms say?

The Big 4 hold platform partnerships — Deloitte with Microsoft, Accenture with AWS, PwC with Google Cloud — that generated over USD 15bn of revenue in 2024 (Gartner). Their advice routes to those platforms 67% of the time, against 23% for independent advisors (Gartner, 2024). We hold no such partnerships and we publish that fact.

Can you support an RFP, including drafting and scoring?

Yes. We draft the RFP requirements, score vendor responses against a written rubric you sign first, and write the ADR documenting the decision. Average ERP selection cycles run nine to twelve months; a Prudentia-led cycle runs eight to twelve weeks.

What does a board-level deliverable look like?

A board-ready memo is one page. It states the decision being asked, two or three options scored against the same criteria, the recommended call, the named risks, and a single reversal point. The longer artefacts — ADRs, the architecture views, the TCO model — sit behind the memo. We do not deliver decks unless you explicitly want one.

What if our team disagrees with you?

The ADR records the alternatives considered and the constraint that ruled them out. If your team disagrees, the ADR is the document we update — not the position we defend. We will not win an architecture debate; we write down what was decided and why, so the next engineer can read it in three years.
