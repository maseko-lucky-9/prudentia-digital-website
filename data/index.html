Capability 02

Multi-source pipeline build

The problem. Data is scattered across point-of-sale, enterprise resource planning, customer relationship management, and ops systems. Analysts spend half their week reconciling spreadsheets. Pipeline freshness is measured in days, not minutes — and the source-to-dashboard chain breaks every time a vendor changes its export format.

What we build. Hosted ingestion via Fivetran or self-hosted Airbyte; custom connectors for legacy systems. Quality failures block the pipeline before they reach a dashboard.

Example use case. A multi-store retailer consolidates six legacy POS systems into one BigQuery warehouse with a Looker semantic layer. Transaction latency drops from a day to minutes; weekly reconciliation is eliminated; cross-format checkout layout decisions lift complementary product purchases 15%.

Under the hood. Fivetran or Airbyte, Postgres / BigQuery, dbt with contract tests, Dagster / Airflow orchestration, freshness SLAs per dataset.

How We Ship

Production data is a different discipline.

Quality gates from day one

Every dbt model ships with tests on uniqueness, referential integrity, and freshness. Failures block the pipeline before they reach a dashboard. The dashboard never lies because the pipeline can't lie.

Tests before dashboards.

Lineage is documented, not implied

Every metric traces from dashboard to semantic layer to dbt model to source table to operator system. When someone asks "where does this number come from?" you can answer in seconds, not days of forensic reconstruction.

Every number, traceable.

Freshness SLAs per dataset

Real-time isn't always needed; daily isn't always enough. We define freshness service-level agreements (SLAs) per dataset — 15 minutes for ops, 24 hours for commercial — and alert when they slip.

Fresh enough, no fresher.

Schema versioning with contracts

Schemas change. We use dbt's versioning and contract patterns to evolve schemas without breaking downstream consumers. No mystery dashboard breakages on the Monday morning after a release.

Change without breaking.

Privacy by design — POPIA defaults

Personally identifiable information is tokenised at the staging layer. Row-level security enforces who-sees-what at the warehouse, not the dashboard. PII never reaches a downstream consumer unless the policy permits it.

Privacy defaults, not afterthoughts.

Common Questions

Answers before you book

How long does a data engagement take?

A Data Audit is one working day, fixed-fee, with a written readout delivered inside two weeks of the call. A dashboard or pipeline build runs four to eight weeks, fixed-fee per phase, with a working data product at the end. Analytics Retainers are monthly with no fixed end date — most clients run them for at least two quarters.

How do you handle our customers' data under POPIA?

Personally identifiable information is tokenised at the staging layer; only downstream consumers with a documented policy reason ever see raw values. Row-level security enforces access at the warehouse, not the dashboard. Retention is policy-driven, not folklore. The Information Regulator's 2024 enforcement track — three POPIA enforcement notices, the first administrative fine on a government department, and 30+ PAIA assessments — is the floor we engineer above.

Which BI tool do you use — Power BI, Looker, Metabase, Tableau?

The one that fits. Microsoft-shop, Power BI. Self-hosted economy, Metabase. Governed semantic layer at scale, Looker. Tableau for visual-analyst teams already invested in it. We have no licence kickback bias, and we publish our trade-off note before we recommend. Forrester's TEI for Power BI cites $3.70 returned per $1 spent when adoption is structured around real use cases — not when the licence is bought first and the use case is invented later.

What if three teams report three different numbers for the same KPI?

That is exactly what the audit fixes. The deliverable is one canonical definition per metric — versioned, governed, documented in a semantic layer with dbt tests on uniqueness and referential integrity. Conflict moves from "whose number is right" to "what should our shared definition be." The average mid-market organisation reconciles three to five competing "sources of truth" for the same KPI; we don't merge them, we replace them with one.

Can you maintain this after launch, or do we need to hire a data team?

Either works. Analytics Retainers cover pipelines, dashboards, machine-learning model retraining, and quarterly quality reviews on a monthly cadence. Hiring economics typically cross over with a retainer at the 12-to-18-month mark for a fully-loaded senior data engineer. When that point is approaching, we'll tell you — and we'll help you write the job spec and shape the handover.

Can we self-host instead of using a cloud warehouse?

Yes. Postgres, dbt, and Metabase run on your infrastructure with no per-seat licensing penalty. We pick the deployment that fits your data-residency and cost posture — not ours. Serverless Postgres covers most mid-market workloads under 500GB at a fraction of cloud-warehouse cost, and it sidesteps the worst of the SA National Data and Cloud Policy localisation risk for sovereign data.
