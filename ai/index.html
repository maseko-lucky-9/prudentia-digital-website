Capability 01

Agent Loops

The problem. Your team runs the same multi-step workflow every day — research a supplier, build a brief, route an approval, generate a report. Each cycle costs four to six hours of senior time, and you can't hire your way out of the volume.

What we build. Agents that hold state across steps, call your tools (CRM, file storage, internal APIs), make decisions at branch points, and finish a ten-step workflow unattended. State is persisted so a crash mid-run resumes cleanly. Every action is logged for audit.

Example use case. A mid-sized law firm's due-diligence intake: receive a brief, pull counterparty filings from CIPC, summarise material risks, draft a client memo, and route it for partner review. Cycle time: 4 hours → 18 minutes. Partner reviews 20 briefs a week instead of 5.

Under the hood. LangGraph for stateful orchestration, Claude Agent SDK for lighter tool-use loops, durable checkpointing, OpenTelemetry traces, integration with your existing identity provider.

Capability 02

Production RAG

The problem. Your team can't find the right policy, contract clause, or precedent in your own document library. Search is keyword-only. Institutional knowledge lives in five people's heads. New hires take six months to ramp.

What we build. A retrieval-augmented generation system that answers questions from your private documents, with citations back to the source page. Hybrid retrieval (vector + keyword + reranking). On-premise or cloud, your choice. POPIA-aware data handling baked in.

Example use case. A financial-services compliance team with 50,000 pages of regulator correspondence, internal policy, and audit findings. Analyst asks "what's our position on Section 12J carry-forward losses?" — gets a cited answer in 4 seconds, with the source PDF and page number. Previously: half a day of manual searching.

Under the hood. sqlite-vec or pgvector for retrieval, Claude or local LLMs for generation, cross-encoder reranking, citation-grounded responses, recall@5 ≥ 0.85 measured before release.

Capability 03

Evals

The problem. You're paying for AI but can't prove it's getting better. A model vendor pushes an update and your output quality silently drops. There's no test suite. There's no leaderboard. You're flying blind.

What we build. A 50-example evaluation harness scoring accuracy, hallucination rate, latency, and cost per request. Every model change runs the suite. A regression of more than five percent fails CI and alerts you. Dashboard shows the leaderboard across Claude, GPT, Gemini, and local models for your task.

Example use case. A fintech using AI to triage support tickets. Vendor updates the model. Eval suite catches a 12% drop in correct triage on financial-product queries within an hour. Old model is pinned, vendor is notified, no customer ever sees the regression.

Under the hood. Braintrust for experiment tracking and dashboards, custom eval sets versioned in git, GitHub Actions for regression gating, automated alerts via your existing channels.

Capability 04

Vector Databases

The problem. Customers and staff phrase the same question ten different ways. Keyword search only finds the ones with the exact wording. Tickets get misrouted, contract clauses get missed, knowledge base articles go unread.

What we build. Semantic search that finds by meaning, not exact words. Same query language as your existing search; results that actually match user intent. We pick the right vector store for your data sensitivity, scale, and existing stack — not the trendy one.

Example use case. A B2B SaaS support team's knowledge base. "How do I cancel?" and "I want to leave" and "stop my subscription" all return the same article. Self-serve deflection rate up from 18% to 47%, two FTEs reclaimed for higher-value support.

Under the hood. PostgreSQL with pgvector (default — keeps data in your existing database), Pinecone (managed, fast), or Weaviate (self-hosted, multi-tenant). Each has a published trade-off note we share before we recommend.

Capability 05

MCP — Model Context Protocol

The problem. Your developers want AI in the tools they already use — Claude Desktop, Cursor, internal IDEs — not yet another standalone chatbot tab. Building bespoke integrations for every tool is unsustainable.

What we build. Production MCP servers that expose your data and operations as callable tools to any MCP-compatible client. One server, every client. Anthropic-shipped open standard, adopted by every major AI lab.

Example use case. A property-management firm exposes its lease database, maintenance system, and CIPC company-lookup as an MCP server. Staff query "all leases expiring in the next 30 days for portfolio X, with current arrears" inside Claude Desktop. No new UI to learn. Internal tool adoption goes from 30% to 90%.

Under the hood. MCP TypeScript and Python SDKs, stdio for local clients, Streamable HTTP for remote, OAuth and token auth, OpenTelemetry tracing, rate limiting, and a health endpoint that wakes us up before our customers do.

Common Questions

Answers before you book

How long does an AI engagement take?

An AI Audit is one working day, finished within two weeks of the strategy session. A Capability Pilot runs four to six weeks, with a working system at the end. Retainers are monthly with no fixed end date — most clients run them for at least two quarters.

Do you sign NDAs?

Yes. We send a counter-signed mutual NDA within 24 hours of a strategy session, before any of your confidential data is shared. If you have your own NDA template, we'll review and counter-sign that instead.

Where does our data live during a pilot? Is it POPIA-compliant?

Your data either stays in environments you control (we deploy into your VPC or on-premises) or lives in an isolated workspace we host for the engagement. Default retention is seven days unless you extend it under a signed pilot scope. Each engagement gets its own isolated workspace — never shared across clients. POPIA-regulated personal information is only ingested with explicit lawful basis. Full handling rules are in our demo-data policy.

What does pricing look like?

Three tiers — AI Audit, Capability Pilot, and AI Engagement Retainer. Indicative ranges are shared in the strategy session after we understand your scope. We price on outcome and scope, not hourly, because hourly pricing rewards slow delivery and punishes the parts we've automated. Audits are fixed-fee, pilots are fixed-fee, retainers are monthly.

Can we self-host?

Yes — and we default to self-hostable architectures. PostgreSQL with pgvector for retrieval, locally-runnable LLMs (Llama, Qwen, others) where they meet quality bars, on-premise document pipelines. Cloud-hosted is supported where it makes operational sense. Vendor portability is built in: you can swap the model layer without rewriting the application.

What if the model vendor changes pricing, deprecates a model, or shuts down?

This is why we architect for portability. Model choice is abstracted behind an interface, so you can move from Anthropic to OpenAI to a local model without touching application code. The eval harness verifies behaviour stays correct after a swap. We've designed for this from the first commit — it's not a retrofit.
