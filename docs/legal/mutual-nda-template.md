When approved, this becomes the canonical template. The workflow is:

## 1. Purpose

The Parties wish to discuss a possible business relationship relating to the design, build, deployment, and operation of AI engineering systems for [PROSPECT_SHORT_NAME] (the "**Purpose**"). In connection with the Purpose, each Party may disclose confidential information to the other.

## 2. Definition of Confidential Information

"**Confidential Information**" means any information disclosed by one Party (the "**Discloser**") to the other (the "**Recipient**"), in any form (oral, written, visual, electronic, machine-readable), that:

(a) is marked or identified as confidential at the time of disclosure, or
(b) should reasonably be understood as confidential given its nature and the circumstances of disclosure.

Confidential Information includes, without limitation: technical data, trade secrets, source code, model prompts, training data, evaluation datasets, business plans, customer lists, pricing, financial information, strategic plans, and personal information (as defined in the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 — "**POPIA**").

## 3. Exclusions

Confidential Information does **not** include information that the Recipient can demonstrate:

(a) was rightfully in its possession, without obligation of confidentiality, before disclosure by the Discloser;
(b) is or becomes publicly available through no act or omission of the Recipient;
(c) is rightfully received from a third party who is not under an obligation of confidentiality to the Discloser;
(d) is independently developed by the Recipient without use of or reference to the Discloser's Confidential Information; or
(e) is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, **provided** the Recipient gives the Discloser prompt written notice (where lawful) so that the Discloser may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

## 4. Obligations

For so long as Confidential Information remains confidential, the Recipient shall:

(a) **use** the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;
(b) **protect** it with the same degree of care as it uses for its own confidential information of similar importance, and in no case less than a **reasonable standard of care**;
(c) **limit access** to those of its employees, contractors, professional advisers, and affiliates ("**Representatives**") who need to know it for the Purpose and who are bound by written or professional confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement;
(d) **not copy, reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble** Confidential Information except as strictly necessary for the Purpose; and
(e) **be responsible** for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.

The Recipient shall not be required to label or specially mark its internal use of Confidential Information so long as access is restricted in accordance with the obligations of this Section 4.

## 5. POPIA-specific obligations

Where Confidential Information includes personal information regulated by POPIA:

(a) the Recipient processes that personal information **only as the Discloser's Operator** (as defined in POPIA), and **only** as instructed by the Discloser in writing for the Purpose;
(b) the Recipient maintains appropriate, reasonable technical and organisational measures to safeguard personal information against loss, damage, unauthorised destruction, and unlawful access or processing, in accordance with Section 19 of POPIA;
(c) the Recipient notifies the Discloser **without undue delay and in any event within 72 hours** of becoming aware of any personal-information breach;
(d) on termination of the Purpose or on the Discloser's written request (whichever is earlier), the Recipient deletes or returns all personal information and provides written confirmation of deletion.

## 6. Term and duration of obligations

This Agreement enters into force on the Effective Date and may be terminated by either Party on **thirty (30) days' written notice**. Termination does not affect the Parties' obligations with respect to Confidential Information disclosed before termination.

Confidentiality obligations under this Agreement survive for:

- **two (2) years** from the Effective Date for Confidential Information that is not a trade secret or personal information;
- **as long as it remains a trade secret**, for trade secrets;
- **as long as POPIA imposes obligations on the Recipient**, for personal information.

## 7. No license, no warranty

This Agreement does not grant the Recipient any licence, right, or interest in any patent, copyright, trade mark, trade secret, or other intellectual property of the Discloser, except the limited right to use Confidential Information for the Purpose.

All Confidential Information is disclosed "**as is**". The Discloser makes no warranty as to the accuracy, completeness, or fitness for any purpose of the Confidential Information.

## 8. Return or destruction

Upon written request from the Discloser, or upon termination of this Agreement, the Recipient shall promptly:

(a) **return** all Confidential Information in tangible form, or **destroy** it (including all copies and derivatives), at the Discloser's election; and
(b) provide **written confirmation** of return or destruction signed by a duly authorised representative.

The Recipient may retain one (1) archival copy solely for legal or regulatory compliance purposes, and may retain backups in routine secure backup systems until they expire in the ordinary course, **provided** that retained copies remain subject to the obligations of this Agreement.

## 9. Remedies

The Parties acknowledge that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Each Party is therefore entitled to seek **injunctive or interdictory relief** in addition to any other remedy available at law or in equity.

## 10. No obligation to proceed

Nothing in this Agreement obliges either Party to enter into any further business relationship. Either Party may terminate discussions at any time without liability, subject only to the obligations of this Agreement.

## 11. Independent Parties

The Parties are independent contractors. This Agreement creates no agency, partnership, joint venture, or employment relationship.

## 12. Assignment

Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other, except to a successor in a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets.

## 13. Governing law and jurisdiction

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the **Republic of South Africa**. The Parties consent to the non-exclusive jurisdiction of the **High Court of South Africa, Gauteng Division** for any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, save that either Party may apply for urgent or interdictory relief in any court of competent jurisdiction.

## Implementation notes (not part of the legal document)

Standard placeholders that get filled in per-engagement:

- `[EFFECTIVE_DATE]` — date of execution (typically same day as the strategy session)
- `[PRUDENTIA_ADDRESS]` — Prudentia's CIPC-registered address
- `[PROSPECT_LEGAL_NAME]` — full registered name
- `[PROSPECT_ENTITY_TYPE]` — "private company", "close corporation", "public benefit organisation", "trust", etc.
- `[PROSPECT_REG_NUMBER]` — CIPC registration number
- `[PROSPECT_ADDRESS]` — registered address
- `[PROSPECT_SHORT_NAME]` — convenient short name used throughout (e.g., "Acme")
- `[PROSPECT_CONTACT_EMAIL]` / `[PROSPECT_CONTACT_NAME]` / `[PROSPECT_CONTACT_TITLE]` — for notices
- `[PROSPECT_SIGNATORY_NAME]` / `[PROSPECT_SIGNATORY_TITLE]` — who's actually signing for the prospect

Other variations to consider with legal:

- **3-year survival** for highly sensitive industries (financial services, healthcare) — the 2-year default is on the lighter end of South African practice.
- **Liquidated damages clause** for trade-secret breach — optional; some prospects expect it.
- **Mutual indemnification** — optional; some larger prospects' templates include it. Keep it symmetric if so.
- **Export-control / sanctions warranty** — only relevant if dealing with US clients or US-sanctioned-country exposure. Add if relevant.
- **Source-code escrow / handover** — for Capability Pilot engagements, sometimes a clause that prospect-paid deliverables are escrowed; keep out of the NDA, put in the engagement scope.
