## 1. Market sizing

The digital-transformation advisory market is the single largest professional-services category in the world and the slowest-recovering one in South Africa. The shape of the demand — sustained spend against a 70% failure rate — is what makes the category interesting.

### Global advisory spend

- **Digital-transformation consulting market: USD 238.4bn in 2024, projected USD 460.3bn by 2034, CAGR ~6.8%** [C1]. Other analyst ranges sit between USD 88bn (narrow definition) and USD 344.7bn (broad definition) [C2, C3] — the variance is methodological, not contradictory. Treat the USD 238bn–USD 345bn band as the canonical headline.
- **Big 4 advisory revenue: USD 95.4bn (2023), now larger than audit at every Big 4 firm** [C4]. Advisory is where the margin lives; vendor partnerships supply ~USD 15bn of that revenue (see §5).
- **Gartner published its first Magic Quadrant for Digital Technology and Business Consulting Services in January 2026**, naming Accenture, Deloitte and BCG as Leaders [C5, C6, C7]. The category now has its own analyst coverage — it was not a separate quadrant until this year.

### SA advisory spend

- **SA management consulting market: USD 1.97bn (2026), forecast USD 2.46bn by 2031** (CAGR ~4.5%) [C8].
- **SA IT consulting & implementation market: USD 343.84m in 2025** [C9].
- **SA digital infrastructure spend tipped to hit R1.2tn by 2030** [C10] — but business IT spend growth has slowed significantly versus global averages [C11], creating budget pressure that forces sharper procurement and harder vendor questions.
- **Mid-market SA businesses typically spend R5m–R20m annually on transformation initiatives** [C11].

### The 70% failure stat (canonical)

The single most-cited number in the category, and the one Prudentia's advisory positioning leans on:

- **McKinsey: ~70% of large-scale transformations fail to achieve their objectives** [C12, C13]. McKinsey estimates roughly USD 900bn of the USD 1.3tn invested in transformation in a single year was wasted [C12].
- **BCG (replicated finding): only 30% of digital transformations meet or exceed expectations on timeline, budget, and scope** [C14, C15]. BCG's six success factors (integrated strategy, leadership commitment, talent, agile governance, monitoring, technology/data architecture) can flip the odds to 80% [C16].
- **88% of projects with excellent change management hit their objectives, vs. 13% with poor or absent change management** [C17]. Implication: the differentiator is not the technology choice — it is the discipline around the technology choice.

### The rise of "small senior team" advisory

A structural shift the page should name:

- **In 2024 the industry moved toward extreme specialisation. Boutique firms increasingly compete with the Big 4 on the strength of senior-led delivery rather than scale** [C18]. The pattern: founder or principal consultant on every engagement, no junior delegation.
- **In 2025 PE-backed advisory firms (e.g., Unity Advisory) launched explicitly to stay out of audit and platform-reseller agreements** [C19]. The vendor-neutrality narrative is now a recognised market position, not a curiosity.

## 2. Pain-point catalogue

Eleven distinct pains across the four target ICPs. Each is paraphrased from a named source — none are invented.

### SA-FS (mid-market lenders, insurers, fintechs)

1. **"S/4HANA migration is eating our budget — and AI is next in line."** 61% of SAP customers cite budget constraints as the largest obstacle; 48% struggle with integration; nearly 60% of completed S/4HANA programmes overran their planned schedule [C20, C21]. SA-FS clients deciding between core-banking modernisation paths face this directly.
2. **"We are deciding what to build vs. buy vs. modernise and there is no neutral voice in the room."** A 2024 Gartner survey found 67% of organisations using vendor-led advisory adopted the vendor's own platform as primary infrastructure, versus 23% of organisations using independent advisory who chose based on workload fit [C19]. Vendor-led advice routes to vendor-built solutions.
3. **"Our PA Joint Standard 2 deadline is June 2026 and our cybersecurity governance is not board-ready."** FSCA/PA Joint Standard 2 on Cybersecurity and Cyber Resilience came into force on 1 June 2025; financial institutions have one year to achieve full compliance [C22, C23]. Banks, insurers, retirement funds and FSPs all in scope.
4. **"SARB wants a board-approved data and cloud strategy — we have neither."** SARB Directive D3/2018 requires banks to maintain a formal board-approved data strategy and governance framework before any material cloud arrangement [C24]. The 2025 SARB consultation paper proposes tightened localisation requirements [C25].

### SMB (founder-led, R20m+ turnover)

5. **"We know we need to modernise but we don't know where to start."** Resource limitations, digital-skills gaps and infrastructure constraints are documented as the three biggest barriers for SA SMBs; 78% of SMBs say digital technology plays an important role in their business and 81% see cloud as essential — but adoption of automated inventory, digital payments and IoT lags [C26, C27]. The gap between intent and execution is the buying signal.
6. **"Our SaaS bill grew but no one mapped what we actually use."** Gartner: 25% of SaaS budget is lost to unused entitlements; mid-sized companies typically know only 60–70% of the SaaS tools they have deployed [C28]. A 90-day audit recovers 15–25% of spend at this scale [C28].
7. **"We had one technical co-founder and they left. Now no one can describe our architecture."** No formal documentation of architectural decisions, no written ADRs, no successor brief. Empirical evidence: ADRs improve transparency, traceability, and stakeholder alignment, and reduce onboarding time on takeover engagements [C29, C30].

### SA-Mid (mid-market enterprises in stalled multi-year programmes)

8. **"We've been transforming for three years and we're still on legacy SAP."** By end-2024 only 39% of the ~35,000 SAP ECC customers globally had migrated to S/4HANA; 40%+ are expected to stay on legacy ECC beyond 2027 [C20]. SA-Mid clients in this cohort are not exceptional — they are typical, and they are spending without measurable progress.
9. **"Our vendor RFP took nine months and we chose wrong."** ERP selection cycles realistically take 4–6 months for mid-market and 12+ months for complex enterprise; 68% of ERP selection projects fail before implementation begins [C31]. Average cost overrun: 189%; in discrete manufacturing, 215% with a 73% failure rate [C31].
10. **"No one will tell us the truth."** McKinsey finds 60%+ of respondents who report stalled digital transformations attribute the cause to factors organisations can control — but ~40% point to hierarchical cultures and siloed working as the killer of momentum [C13, C32]. The truth-telling problem is a governance problem, not an information problem.

### INT (acquirer / scaling-into-SA)

11. **"We are buying a SA business and we don't know the technology risk."** SA M&A due-diligence requires technology-specific assessment of IT capability, BEE compliance impact on key licences, exchange-control compliance with SARB, and ESG/POPIA posture — none of which off-the-shelf Big 4 due-diligence frameworks evaluate as a single, written, decision-grade artefact [C33].

## 3. ROI benchmarks

Advisory ROI is harder to source than implementation ROI because the deliverable is a decision, not a system. We anchor on three measurable framings.

### A. Total cost of ownership (TCO) reduction via architecture review

- **20–30% IT-cost reduction is the documented range for enterprise architecture reviews** [C34]. Within this:
  - Platform standardisation reduces integration cost, support complexity, vendor sprawl [C34].
  - Licence optimisation: **30–50% reduction in software spend** [C34].
  - Infrastructure simplification: **15–25% infrastructure cost reduction + 20–40% operational-efficiency gain** [C34].
  - Cloud optimisation: over-provisioning wastes 30–40% of cloud spend on average; combined strategies reduce cloud spend by **40–50% without sacrificing capability** [C34].
- **Legacy modernisation case evidence: 38–52% TCO reduction is documented for full programmes; one mid-sized European bank saved 38% in 18 months with phased migration and 62% faster time-to-market** [C35].

### B. Programme rescue: cost of intervention vs. cost of inaction

- **Initial rapid-assessment engagements: GBP 12k–28k (≈ R280k–R650k) for 2–3 week codebase + architecture + realistic-completion-cost reviews** [C36]. Sized to the deliverable, not the firm.
- **Case 1 (property-management software): rescue cost GBP 180k + sunk GBP 520k = GBP 700k vs. GBP 760k rebuild. Net saving GBP 60k, plus five months earlier revenue realisation and GBP 240k annual operational savings** [C36].
- **Case 2 (financial services): rescue investment GBP 380k prevented complete loss of GBP 680k sunk; salvaged GBP 180k of value; total value preservation > GBP 800k** [C36].
- **The "cost of inaction" framing for boards: large enterprises that delay comprehensive transformation face 15–25% higher operational costs than digital leaders in their sector** [C37].

### C. Vendor-selection cost avoidance

- **Choosing the wrong vendor costs 12–18 months and millions in budget overruns** [C31].
- **Vendor-led advisory: 67% of organisations adopted the vendor's own platform regardless of fit; independent advisory: 23%** [C19]. The R-value of this on a R20m ERP selection: the difference between a 4-month and a 12-month decision cycle, and the difference between a 40% over-spec licence and a fit-tested one.
- **SaaS-sprawl audit: 15–25% of annual SaaS spend recoverable in 90 days** [C28]. For a mid-market client at R8m/year SaaS spend, that is R1.2m–R2m recovered without losing a tool.

### D. Time-to-decision compression

- **Structured 48-hour decision protocols (executive use): hour 0–6 framing + hour 6–24 scenario building** [C38]. Where a board normally takes three months to commission a vendor, an Advisory engagement compresses that to two weeks of structured comparison with written ADRs.
- **Organisations using RACI/DACI properly report 25% faster cycle times and 70% fewer "who decides" questions** [C39].

What the market does not provide: **a small senior team, written-deliverable, vendor-neutral advisor that produces ADRs and architecture artefacts a board can sign and an in-house team can execute against without a multi-year retainer.** Each layer above misses one of these:

Prudentia is the written-output, vendor-neutral, senior-led advisory that bridges strategy and engineering. The phrase to use on the page: *"the in-house architect you wish you had — for a fixed scope, with written ADRs."*

## 5. Regulatory framing

Advisory engagements produce ADRs, roadmaps and written governance artefacts — not data flows. Direct POPIA exposure is limited. But the regulatory frame is the lens through which clients evaluate every advisory recommendation.

### POPIA (mandatory for all SA work)

- **POPIA imposes administrative fines up to R10m for non-compliance** [C47]. Enforcement is active: 2023–2025 fines include R5m against the Department of Justice (2023), R5m against the Department of Basic Education (2024), R500k against Blouberg Municipality (2025), R100k against Lancet Laboratories (2025), R100k against FT Rams Consulting (2025) [C48, C49].
- **The Information Regulator's new compliance monitoring programme (2025) requires organisations to demonstrate compliance through documentation, internal controls and governance processes** — a shift to ongoing supervision rather than incident-only enforcement [C50].
- **Advisory implication:** every roadmap must surface a data-lineage and data-residency view. Every vendor evaluation must include POPIA operator status and breach-notification obligations.

### Banking-grade controls (SA-FS in scope)

- **FSCA/PA Joint Standard 2 of 2024 on Cybersecurity and Cyber Resilience** — effective 1 June 2025, full compliance required by 1 June 2026 [C22, C23]. Banks, insurers, retirement funds, FSPs and investment-fund administrators are in scope. Mandates periodic audits, cybersecurity governance, third-party oversight, incident recovery plans.
- **SARB Directive D3/2018 — Cloud Computing and Data Offshoring by Banks** — requires board-approved data strategy + governance framework; banks must inform SARB of material cloud arrangements [C24].
- **SARB Cybersecurity and Cyber-Resilience Directive within the National Payment System** — payment-system participants have parallel obligations [C51].
- **2025 SARB consultation paper** proposes localisation mandates for cloud computing and data offshoring [C25] — a moving regulatory target advisory clients need a view on.

### Governance frameworks named in scope

- **TOGAF 10** — global enterprise-architecture framework. SA's national architecture efforts draw on TOGAF principles. Increasingly a procurement signal in SA banking [C52, C53]. Real IRM Solutions is the SA TOGAF certification provider [C46].
- **ISO 9001 (and the FDIS 9001:2026 revision)** — Quality Management Systems with new emphasis on digital readiness, AI/automation governance, cybersecurity awareness within QMS scope [C54, C55].
- **DACI vs. RACI** — DACI (Driver / Approver / Contributor / Informed) is increasingly preferred over RACI for transformation programmes because RACI does not name a Driver and stalls are usually driver-absence failures [C39].
- **RAPID** — for steering-committee and cross-functional design decisions [C39].
- **Compression Planning** (6-step visual planning) — used by Fortune 500s for high-output strategic decision-making in compressed timeframes [C38].

### International (INT-tier engagements)

- **GDPR** (EU/UK customer of SA business): lawful basis, data minimisation, DSR rights, breach notification ≤ 72hrs.
- **SOC2 / ISO 27001** — control frameworks for service organisations. Required for INT enterprise + SA-FS procurement.

## 7. Five-capability brainstorm

Less stack-heavy than other Prudentia services. Emphasis on written deliverables, frameworks, decision artefacts.

### Capability 1 — Transformation Consult (90-minute strategic)

- **Problem.** "We are about to spend R20m on a transformation programme and we want a senior, neutral view before we sign."
- **Solution.** One 90-minute structured session with the founder. Inputs: current architecture diagram, vendor shortlist, board mandate, budget envelope. Outputs: one-page memo with the three questions the board has not yet asked, two named risks, one recommended decision sequence, and a written follow-up.
- **Use case.** A SA-Mid retailer's executive committee paused a R28m ERP signing after a 90-minute consult surfaced an unresolved data-residency conflict with SARB D3/2018.
- **Stack / frameworks.** Compression Planning, DACI, written one-pager.

### Capability 2 — Architecture Review with written ADRs

- **Problem.** "Our architecture has no architect. Decisions have been made but never written down. New engineers re-litigate them every quarter."
- **Solution.** A four-to-six-week engagement. We document the current system in TOGAF-aligned views, surface every undocumented decision, and produce a written set of Architecture Decision Records — one per material call. Each ADR names the alternatives considered, the constraint, the decision, and the reversal cost.
- **Use case.** A SA-FS lender's microservices estate had 47 services and zero ADRs. After the review, 31 ADRs were committed to a private repo; the next architect onboarded in two weeks instead of two months.
- **Stack / frameworks.** TOGAF 10, ADR (joelparkerhenderson template), AWS Prescriptive Guidance ADR process, written deliverables only.

### Capability 3 — Cloud Migration Strategy & Vendor Selection

- **Problem.** "We are choosing between AWS, Azure and GCP and every conversation routes us toward the vendor's own platform."
- **Solution.** A vendor-neutral evaluation that ignores feature parity and benchmark theatre. We score workloads on control, cost behaviour, operations, team skillset and organisational reality. Output: an ADR documenting the decision, a TCO model, a phased migration plan with reversal points, and a SARB D3/2018-ready board memo.
- **Use case.** A SA insurer reduced projected three-year cloud spend by 34% by avoiding a vendor-led, all-platform commit and choosing a hybrid AWS-primary, on-prem-resident pattern.
- **Stack / frameworks.** AWS Well-Architected, Azure Cloud Adoption Framework, written TCO model, ADRs, SARB-aligned governance memo.

### Capability 4 — Legacy Modernisation Roadmap

- **Problem.** "We've been transforming for three years and we are still on legacy SAP. The board has lost patience."
- **Solution.** A six-week diagnostic. We document every system, map every integration, quantify technical debt and TCO, and produce a phased modernisation roadmap with what to replace first, in what order, at what cost, with what expected business impact. The plan names exit criteria, reversal points, and success metrics at each stage.
- **Use case.** A SA-Mid manufacturer's stalled SAP ECC → S/4HANA programme restarted on a phased plan that reduced 18-month residual cost by 38% and named a single Driver per workstream (DACI).
- **Stack / frameworks.** TOGAF, DACI, written roadmap with reversal points, ADR per material decision.

### Capability 5 — Steering Retainer (monthly cadence)

- **Problem.** "Our programme is moving. We want a senior, neutral voice in the steering committee once a month — to push back, ask the unpopular question, write the ADR no one wants to write."
- **Solution.** A monthly fixed-fee retainer. Founder attends one steering meeting per month, reviews two artefacts (proposal, vendor response, design doc) per month, and writes one ADR per quarter on a strategic decision. Exit criteria documented at signing.
- **Use case.** A SA-FS fintech retained a senior advisor for nine months across a payments-modernisation programme. Programme exited the steering retainer when the named exit criteria were met. Cost was 6% of programme spend.
- **Stack / frameworks.** Steering charter, DACI, monthly ADR cadence, written exit criteria.

## 8. Six "how we ship" principles

Discipline-correct lexicon from `_voice-guide.md` (advisory row). All six are written commitments — no PowerPoint, no slogans.

1. **Discovery-first.** No recommendation without a current-state document we co-signed. We do not recommend what we have not seen.
2. **Vendor neutrality.** No platform reseller agreements, no implementation fees from named vendors, no kickback structures. If the right answer is "do not buy", we will write that.
3. **Written ADRs, always.** Every material decision becomes an Architecture Decision Record in a repository you own. Alternatives, constraint, decision, reversal cost. One page each. Searchable in three years.
4. **Measurable success criteria.** Every engagement names its success metric at scoping, not at delivery. If the metric is "the board can approve the cloud RFP this quarter", we write that.
5. **Change-management plan and exit criteria documented.** We do not leave the engagement when the consult ends — we leave when the named exit criteria are met. If neither party can write the exit criteria, we do not start.
6. **Steering cadence by contract.** Monthly retainers have a written steering charter. The charter names the Driver (DACI), the meeting cadence, the artefacts in scope, and the reversal point. No standing retainer without a steering charter.

## 9. FAQ (6 Q&A)

Voice: calmest, most measured. Average sentence ≤ 20 words.

**Q1. How long is a typical engagement?**
Three options, each scoped at signing. A Transformation Consult is one to three days of senior time, output is a written memo. A Roadmap & ADRs engagement runs four to six weeks. A Steering Retainer is monthly, with documented exit criteria.

**Q2. Do you implement, or just advise?**
We advise and we write. We do not implement, and we do not earn fees from the vendors you choose. If you need an implementation partner, the ADR we produce names two or three candidates by capability fit and your team selects.

**Q3. How is "vendor neutrality" different from what Big 4 firms say?**
The Big 4 hold platform partnerships — Deloitte with Microsoft, Accenture with AWS, PwC with Google Cloud — that generated over USD 15bn of revenue in 2024 (source: Gartner). Their advice routes to those platforms 67% of the time, against 23% for independent advisors (Gartner, 2024). We hold no such partnerships and we publish that fact.

**Q4. Can you support an RFP, including drafting and scoring?**
Yes. We draft the RFP requirements, score vendor responses against a written rubric you sign first, and write the ADR documenting the decision. Average ERP selection cycles run nine to twelve months; a Prudentia-led cycle runs eight to twelve weeks.

**Q5. What does a board-level deliverable look like?**
A board-ready memo is one page. It states the decision being asked, two or three options scored against the same criteria, the recommended call, the named risks, and a single reversal point. The longer artefacts — ADRs, the architecture views, the TCO model — sit behind the memo. We do not deliver decks unless you explicitly want one.

**Q6. What if our team disagrees with you?**
The ADR records the alternatives considered and the constraint that ruled them out. If your team disagrees, the ADR is the document we update — not the position we defend. We will not "win" an architecture debate; we will write down what was decided and why, so the next engineer can read it in three years.
